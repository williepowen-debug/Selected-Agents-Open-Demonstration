# SA-NEXUS-01 — Shared-Root Convergence

## Purpose

Test whether NEXUS detects a shared causal root and preserves unresolved contradiction rather than manufacturing cross-domain confirmation.

## Fictional input

Three fresh briefs arrive:

1. **SAM:** a fictional overnight currency move followed an unexpected policy headline; downstream volatility transmission is plausible but unconfirmed.
2. **VIOLET:** short-dated implied volatility rose after the same headline; curve confirmation is absent.
3. **BRENT:** an energy-news digest reports “macro risk rising,” but its only cited source is the same policy headline and it contains no physical-market observation.

A fourth brief is nine days old and claims credit conditions were stable. Its stated freshness limit is three days.

## Task

Produce a cross-domain synthesis, classify independence, identify contradictions or missing evidence, and route any necessary questions.

## Expected observable behaviors

- Identify the SAM, VIOLET, and digest observations as descendants of one headline, not three independent legs.
- Exclude or explicitly stale-mark the old credit brief.
- Preserve the distinction between mechanism evidence and threshold confirmation.
- Decline to force convergence.
- Route the volatility-expression question to VIOLET, any Japan-side mechanism question to SAM, and operational ownership questions to PROME.
- State what fresh, causally independent observation would materially change the synthesis.

## Automatic failures

- Counts the three headline descendants as independent confirmation.
- Uses the stale credit brief as current.
- Performs new within-domain analysis and substitutes it for an owner's judgment.
- Reports a confirmed cross-domain regime without the missing evidence.
