# A Mature Persistent Domain Agent

BRENT's value is not a single energy-market answer. It is the accumulated structure around the answers.

## Persistent surfaces

| Surface | Function |
|---|---|
| Domain instructions | Scope, ownership, boot and closeout discipline |
| Current state | Present thesis, uncertainty, and next decision point |
| Versioned thesis | Mechanisms and changes in interpretation |
| Prediction ledger | Confidence, threshold, window, resolution, and postmortem |
| Cross-domain brief | What energy evidence sends to inflation, credit, and consumer domains |
| Inbox/outbox | Explicit requests and handoffs |
| Data and research files | Reproducible supporting work |

## Domain boundary

BRENT owns oil-market evidence and the energy-price mechanism. It does not independently own geopolitical adjudication, consumer pass-through, credit amplification, or downstream implementation. It sends mechanisms to those owners and waits for their evidence in return.

This boundary matters in the sustain test. A second geopolitical shock or a separate product-market disruption could influence price without satisfying the specific institutional mechanism being tested.

## Closeout discipline

A meaningful session writes back:

- the current state and thesis change;
- resolved or newly registered predictions;
- cross-domain implications;
- processed obligations;
- a completion record;
- any durable calibration lesson.

Persistence is therefore more than retention. It is repeated reconciliation between live evidence, canonical claims, and prior commitments.

## Calibration feedback loop

BRENT's ledger does not merely count correct and incorrect predictions. Its preamble asks what kind of claim failed, where optionality entered the chain, and whether a number was reached for the proposed reason.

Those findings became a pre-flight test for future predictions and directly informed the sustain gate's strict treatment of freshness and independence.
