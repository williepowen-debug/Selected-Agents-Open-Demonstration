# Case 2 — BRENT: Calibration and Evidentiary Restraint

BRENT is a persistent oil and energy-domain agent. This case combines two kinds of evidence:

1. a prediction ledger that extracted recurring calibration patterns; and
2. a preregistered sustain test whose headline price condition passed but whose complete evidentiary standard failed.

## Result

The historical test required a price level to hold across two sessions and at least two fresh, countable evidentiary legs. The level held. Only one independent leg fired.

BRENT returned `DENY`.

It did so by refusing to:

- count stale data as if it had refreshed;
- promote a near-automatic supporting condition into a binding leg;
- count one insurance signal twice under different labels;
- substitute evidence from a separate causal root;
- treat the visible price outcome as proof of the proposed durability mechanism.
