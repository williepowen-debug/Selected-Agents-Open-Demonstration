# Historical Sustain Test — `DENY`

## 1. Preregistration

On July 8, 2026, a sharp energy-market move was judged a possible break in a previously resilient pricing regime. Capital consequences were withheld pending a two-session sustain test.

The specification was ratified on July 9, before the final graded session.

### Confirm rule

All conditions were required:

1. the reference Brent contract settled above **$75** in both sessions;
2. no settlement round-trip below **$74** occurred; and
3. at least **two fresh, countable, independent legs** fired.

`DENY` was defined as the complement of `CONFIRM`; there was no undefined middle.

### Candidate evidence legs

1. A fresh war-risk insurance repricing
2. Sanctions remaining in force
3. A fresh collapse in transits or a new rerouting decision
4. A fresh withdrawal or material restriction of commercial cover

Before the final session, the sanctions leg was explicitly down-weighted because it was close to an automatic pass and therefore had little discriminating power. It could support the conclusion but could not serve as one of the two binding legs.

## 2. Observed level

| Session | Historical settle | Above $75? | Settlement below $74? |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Thursday | Approximately $76 | Yes | No |
| Friday | Approximately $76 | Yes | No |

The level condition passed. It was necessary, not sufficient.

## 3. Evidence count

| Leg | Fresh? | Fired? | Binding? | Reason |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| War-risk repricing | Yes | Yes | Yes | A new post-event repricing was reported |
| Sanctions in force | Yes | Yes | No | Predesignated supporting condition with weak discrimination |
| Transit collapse or new reroute | No qualifying print | No | No | Latest official observation was stale and did not cross the line |
| Commercial cover restriction | No | No | No | Cover remained available; no new restriction was evidenced |

Fresh countable legs: **1**  
Required: **at least 2**

## 4. Independence controls

### No double counting

The war-risk price increase could not also be counted as evidence that commercial cover had been withdrawn. Those were different proposed legs, and the second event had not occurred.

### No stale presumption

An old transit observation could not be treated as if the condition had remained true. No fresh print meant non-countable, not presumed confirmation.

### No causal substitution

A separate disruption in another energy market contributed to the weekly price level but did not belong to the gate's specified causal root. It could not replace a missing institutional leg.

## 5. Verdict

```text
LEVEL: PASS
FRESH INDEPENDENT LEGS: 1 of at least 2
VERDICT: DENY
```

The confidence in `DENY` was high because the arithmetic was unambiguous. Uncertainty about what an unpublished observation might eventually show did not authorize the agent to guess.

## 6. Consequence

The temporary re-escalation was stood down, no downstream construction proposal was produced, and a future re-arm required a second independent fresh leg rather than price alone.

This is the case's central claim: evidentiary restraint changed the decision even after the visible headline condition passed.
